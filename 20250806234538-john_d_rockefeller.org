- **Decisiveness:**
  - Rockefeller never waited for the "right moment"; he created opportunities and acted swiftly.
  - He eliminated the unnecessary to focus on the essential.
  - His environment (office, routines) reflected his drive for efficiency—minimal, distraction-free.

  - Purpose drives discipline, not just structure for the sake of it.
  - Vision sets the tone: he aimed not just for wealth but to dominate the oil industry.

  - Discipline is non-negotiable; routine is liberating, not restrictive; focus must be deliberately practiced.
