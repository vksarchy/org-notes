#+title: Mental Models
#+date: [2025-08-05 Tue 06:52]

Here’s a summary of the **9 powerful mental models** discussed in the video, each with a clear explanation and a simple daily life example:

### 1. **The Map Is Not The Territory**
- **Explanation:** Representations (like models, maps, or plans) are simplified and never fully reflect reality.
- **Daily Life Example:** Your calendar says you have a free day, but unexpected events (traffic, sick child, surprise call) can disrupt your plan. Be flexible and ready to adjust when reality differs from your “map.”

### 2. **Circle of Competence**
- **Explanation:** Know what you truly understand (your circle), and be honest about what you don’t.
- **Daily Life Example:** You love movies but don’t know filmmaking. Don’t try making a film for your business unless you’ve really learned the craft. Instead, hire an expert or learn first.

### 3. **First Principles Thinking**
- **Explanation:** Break down problems to basics and question every assumption.
- **Daily Life Example:** Instead of blindly following popular diet trends, research your body’s needs and build your own meal plan based on nutrients and lifestyle, not just what influencers say.

### 4. **Thought Experiment**
- **Explanation:** Use mental simulations to explore possibilities and test ideas without real risk.
- **Daily Life Example:** Thinking about changing careers? Imagine what your daily routine, finances, and satisfaction would look like a year from now before taking the plunge.

### 5. **Second Order Thinking**
- **Explanation:** Consider not just the immediate impact of your decisions, but the longer-term ripple effects.
- **Daily Life Example:** Eating fast food every day saves time today (first order), but could impact your health, energy, and medical bills over years (second order).

### 6. **Probabilistic Thinking**
- **Explanation:** Choose actions based on the likelihood of different outcomes, not just “will/won’t happen.”
- **Daily Life Example:** There’s a 70% chance of rain, so you bring an umbrella even though it might not rain, balancing preparation with odds.

### 7. **Inversion**
- **Explanation:** Instead of only thinking about what to do, ask what should be avoided or what could go wrong.
- **Daily Life Example:** Planning a vacation? Think, “How could this trip be ruined?” (e.g., losing passport, food poisoning) and make plans to prevent those problems.

### 8. **Occam’s Razor**
- **Explanation:** When faced with multiple explanations, the simplest one is often the correct one.
- **Daily Life Example:** Your phone isn’t working. Before assuming it’s broken, check if it’s simply not charged or switched off.

### 9. **Hanlon’s Razor**
- **Explanation:** Never attribute to malice that which can be explained by stupidity or error.
- **Daily Life Example:** If a friend doesn’t reply to your text, don’t assume they’re angry—maybe they just got busy or forgot.

**Bonus Model:**
**The Eisenhower Matrix (from video’s ending):**
- **Explanation:** Separate tasks by urgency and importance to focus on what really matters.
- **Daily Life Example:** Instead of just reacting to urgent phone calls or emails, block time for important but not urgent tasks—like exercise or planning your goals.

These models help you see life’s patterns more clearly, improve decisions, and avoid common mental traps.
