* Trade System Overview

** What Is Trade in EU4?
Trade is the primary source of income for most nations. Ducats flow through a network of trade nodes, pushed by merchants and trade power. Your goal: collect as much trade value as possible in your home node.

** Core Concepts
*** Trade Node
- Geographic point where trade value accumulates
- All trade happens at nodes â€” control nodes = control income
- Affected by province trade power and merchants

*** Trade Value
- Total ducats available in a node (local production + incoming steered value)
- More value = more money to collect or steer
- Driven by goods produced and production development

*** Trade Power
- Your nation's influence at a node (your share of the pie)
- *Trade Power Share* = your TP Ã· total TP at a node (%)
- Higher share = more ducats collected or steered
- Sources: provinces, light ships, merchants, buildings, trade companies

*** Collecting vs. Steering
- *Collecting* :: Taking ducats out of a node into your treasury
- *Steering (Transferring)* :: Pushing trade value from one node downstream toward another
- Golden rule: *STEER everywhere, COLLECT only at home*

*** Home Node
- Your capital's trade node â€” you auto-collect here for free
- Best collection efficiency â€” always maximize your home node
- Can be changed by moving your trade port (costs 200 diplo points)

*** Merchants
- Agents assigned to nodes to collect or steer trade
- Without merchants, you have no active control of trade flow
- Sources: national ideas, trade ideas, colonies, trade companies

*** Trade Range
- How far your merchants can reach from owned provinces
- Increases with diplo tech, trade ideas, and colonial range

*** Trade Goods
- Each province produces a specific trade good with a market price
- High-value goods (cloth, spices, gold) generate more trade value
- Boosted by manufactories, trade companies, and production development

** Trade Node Mechanics

*** Node Types
- *Upstream Nodes* :: Nodes that feed value INTO a node (e.g., Ivory Coast feeds Sevilla)
- *Downstream Nodes* :: Nodes that receive value FROM a node (you steer downstream toward home)
- *End Nodes* :: Nodes with no outgoing connections â€” value can ONLY be collected here, never leaves
  - English Channel (richest)
  - Genoa (second richest)
  - Venice (third)
- *Inland Nodes* :: Continental nodes with no naval access â€” can't use light ships here

*** Node Value Formula
- Local value + incoming steered value âˆ’ collected/steered out
- Optimize by maximizing inflow and minimizing others' collection

*** Multiple Outflows
- Most nodes can steer to 2â€“3 different downstream nodes
- Your merchants choose WHERE to push the value

*** Caravan Power
- Inland trade power based on total national development
- Large empires get automatic trade power bonus at inland nodes
- Benefits nations like Ming, Ottomans, Mughals

*** Critical Rule
- Trade flows *ONE DIRECTION ONLY* â€” you cannot push trade upstream
- Plan your expansion along trade flow direction

** Trade Power Sources

*** Provincial Trade Power
- Own provinces in a trade node â€” base source of TP
- Conquest is the most reliable way to gain trade power

*** Light Ships
- Each provides ~2â€“3 trade power (scales with naval tech)
- Most flexible and cost-effective TP source
- Assign fleets to "Protect Trade" in key nodes
- 20â€“50 ships is the sweet spot for most nations

*** Merchants
- +2 base trade power at assigned node
- Small but enables collection/steering actions

*** Trade Buildings
- *Marketplace* :: +50% provincial trade power (100 ducats)
- *Trade Depot* :: +100% provincial TP, replaces Marketplace (200 ducats, Tech 17)
- *Stock Exchange* :: +125% provincial TP, replaces Trade Depot (400 ducats, Tech 22)
- Build in highest-base-TP provinces first

*** Centers of Trade
- Provinces with CoT levels: Lv1 (+5 TP), Lv2 (+25 TP), Lv3 (+50 TP)
- Upgrading CoTs is one of the best TP investments per ducat
- Prioritize home node CoTs first

*** Trade Companies
- Assign provinces outside your home subcontinent to a TC
- +100% provincial trade power
- Free merchant at 51%+ TP in the trade company's node
- Crucial for colonial and trade empires

*** Estuaries & Straits
- Natural geographic modifiers on certain provinces (+10â€“25 TP)
- Cannot be created â€” must conquer provinces that have them

*** Transferring Merchant Bonus
- Each merchant in a steering chain adds +5% value to steered trade
- Chain of 3 merchants = +15% value bonus â€” compounds significantly

*** Ideas & Policies
- Trade Ideas, Plutocratic, Diplomatic give global TP bonuses
- Stack with buildings for enormous effect

*** Privateer Fleets
- Send fleets to privateer in rivals' trade nodes
- Steals their TP and gives you Power Projection
- 10â€“20 ships enough to be effective

*** Embargo Rivals
- Diplomatic action against rivals
- Steals trade power at shared nodes â€” completely free, always do it

** Collecting vs. Steering â€” Detailed Rules

*** Collect at Home Node
- Auto-collect with NO merchant needed
- Full trade efficiency, no penalty
- +10% bonus with merchant assigned (optional but good)

*** Collect at Non-Home Node
- Assign merchant to collect at a foreign node
- *-50% trade power penalty* for collecting outside home
- Only do this if you dominate a distant node and can't steer to home

*** Steer (Transfer)
- Assign merchant to push trade value downstream
- No penalty â€” adds steering bonus to value
- *This is your default action for all merchants outside home*

*** Steering Bonus
- Each merchant in a chain adds +5% value to steered trade
- Compounds: 3 merchants in chain = +15% value bonus
- Build the longest chain you can toward your home node

*** Multiple Collection Penalty
- If you collect in 2+ nodes (including home), penalty applies to non-home
- -50% TP in all non-home collection nodes
- Avoid unless you have massive isolated trade power

** Merchant Placement Strategy

*** Rule 1: Maximize Steering Chains
- Place merchants in a continuous chain toward home node
- Example: Bengal â†’ Gujarat â†’ Gulf of Aden â†’ Alexandria â†’ Genoa
- *Highest priority* â€” this is the core strategy

*** Rule 2: Steer High-Value Nodes
- Put merchants at the richest upstream nodes you can reach
- Malacca has huge spice value â€” always steer if you can reach it

*** Rule 3: Compete at Contested Nodes
- Place merchants where rivals have high TP to fight for share
- Only if you have meaningful TP there

*** Rule 4: Don't Waste on Tiny Nodes
- Skip nodes where your TP is <10% â€” minimal return
- Better to focus merchants where you can make a difference

*** Rule 5: Collect at Isolated Rich Nodes (Last Resort)
- Only collect outside home if you dominate and can't steer to home
- Example: Ming collecting at Beijing when value can't reach a better node

*** Rule 6: Colonial Auto-Steer
- Colonial nations automatically steer trade toward overlord
- Colonize for passive trade income â€” no merchant needed

** Trade Efficiency & Income Modifiers

*** Trade Efficiency
- Source: ideas, advisors, tech, traditions
- Multiplier on collected trade income
- +30% TE = 30% more ducats collected
- Stacks additively

*** Trade Steering
- Source: ideas, advisors, policies
- Increases value added when steering trade
- More steering = more value arrives at home node

*** Goods Produced Modifier
- Source: buildings (workshop, manufactory), ideas
- Increases trade value generated by each province
- *Multiplicative with base goods â€” extremely powerful*
- Single most impactful trade modifier in the game

*** Production Efficiency
- Source: ideas, tech, buildings
- Increases production income (NOT trade value)
- Common confusion: this boosts production income, not trade

*** Trade Good Price
- Source: global events, supply/demand
- Higher price = more value per unit produced
- Can't directly control â€” low supply goods are worth more

*** Mercantilism
- Source: events, decisions, DLC mechanics
- Increases provincial trade power, hurts foreign traders
- Higher mercantilism = less TP for foreign merchants in your nodes

** Trade Companies (Dharma DLC)

*** What Are They?
- Assign provinces outside your home subcontinent to a trade company
- +100% provincial trade power
- No manpower from these provinces (trade-off)

*** Extra Merchant
- At 51%+ TP in a TC's trade node, you get a free merchant
- Merchants are scarce â€” this is huge
- Prioritize getting 51% in key TC nodes

*** Trade Company Investments
- Special buildings only for TC provinces
- Governors' Estate (+1 goods produced â€” build first)
- Brokers' Office, Officers' Mess, etc.
- Cost 400â€“1000 ducats each

*** Best TC Regions

- Colonize or conquer coastal provinces

*** TC vs. Full Core
- TC: better for trade (TP bonus) but no manpower
- Full core: gives both TP and manpower
- TC for trade nodes, full core in your heartland
