## Who is Paul Saladino, MD, and What Does He Recommend?

**Paul Saladino, MD** is a double board-certified physician and a prominent advocate of the "animal-based diet." He is known for challenging mainstream nutritional advice and promoting a diet centered on animal foods, with a particular emphasis on nutrient density and the exclusion of processed foods[7][13][15].

## Key Principles and Rationale

- **Whole, Unprocessed Foods:** Saladino strongly advises eliminating ultra-processed foods, arguing these disrupt metabolism and contribute to chronic disease[7].
- **Animal Foods as Nutrient Dense:** He believes meat and organs provide the most bioavailable nutrients for humans.
- **Carbohydrates from Fruit & Honey:** Unlike strict carnivore diets, Saladino includes fruit and honey, citing improved energy, athletic performance, and digestion[7].
- **Plant Defense Chemicals:** He suggests that some people are sensitive to plant toxins and may benefit from minimizing or eliminating certain vegetables, especially if dealing with autoimmune or gut issues[7].
- **Intermittent Fasting:** Saladino has publicly changed his stance, no longer believing fasting or intermittent fasting is necessary for optimal health, and instead focuses on diet quality[9].
