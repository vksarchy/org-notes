Rocket stoves are used in the cooking industry for **fuel-efficient, high-heat cooking**. They are popular in **commercial kitchens, catering services, street food stalls, and eco-friendly restaurants**. Their **main advantages** include:

1. **Fuel Efficiency** – Uses **less fuel** than traditional stoves, making it cost-effective.
2. **High Heat Output** – Generates intense heat quickly, ideal for bulk cooking.
3. **Eco-Friendly** – Produces **less smoke and emissions**, making it cleaner.
4. **Portable & Durable** – Useful in **outdoor catering, disaster relief, and off-grid kitchens**.
5. **Versatile Use** – Suitable for **boiling, frying, baking, and grilling**.

### **Fuel Sources**

Rocket stoves do **not** use **LPG**. Instead, they burn **solid biomass fuels**, such as:

- **Wood** (sticks, twigs, small logs)
- **Charcoal**
- **Agricultural waste** (corn cobs, coconut husks)
- **Compressed biomass pellets**

Some advanced rocket stoves incorporate **gasification technology**, making them even more efficient. They are widely used in **developing countries, outdoor kitchens, and eco-conscious businesses** looking for **sustainable cooking solutions**.
