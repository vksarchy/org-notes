### Example Layouts of Polyhouse Farming:

#### 1. **Standard Tunnel Polyhouse**

- **Structure**: A long, semi-circular, tunnel-shaped polyhouse with a metal or PVC frame covered in UV-resistant polyethylene sheets.
- **Best for**: Growing vegetables like tomatoes, cucumbers, bell peppers, and leafy greens.
- **Irrigation**: Drip irrigation system with water-soluble nutrients.
- **Climate Control**: Manual or automated vent openings for temperature and humidity regulation.
- **Optional Features**: Shade nets for temperature control, insect-proof netting.

#### 2. **Multi-Span Polyhouse**

- **Description**: Multiple interconnected tunnel structures forming a large polyhouse.
- **Best for**: Large-scale commercial farming of high-value crops like exotic vegetables, flowers, and herbs.
- **Features**:
    - Advanced climate control (foggers, ventilation fans, evaporative cooling pads).
    - Hydroponic or aeroponic systems for water-efficient farming.
    - Automated fertigation (nutrient mixing in drip irrigation).

#### 3. **Dome-Shaped or Gothic Arch Polyhouse**

- **Design**: A dome or pointed arch shape, providing better wind resistance and higher headroom.
- **Best for**: Taller plants like cucumber, gourds, or climbing vegetables.
- **Advantages**:
    - Better resistance against strong winds and heavy rains.
    - Less water accumulation on the roof.
    - Improved light penetration for optimal plant growth.

#### 4. **Shade Net House (Low-Cost Polyhouse)**

- **Structure**: Uses a frame covered with shade nets rather than complete plastic sheets.
- **Best for**: Crops that require partial sunlight like flowers (roses, gerberas), certain vegetables (lettuce, spinach), and nurseries.
- **Benefits**:
    - Cost-effective alternative to a fully covered polyhouse.
    - Protects plants from excessive sunlight and wind while maintaining ventilation.

#### 5. **Hybrid Polyhouse with Climate Control**

- **Description**: A modern structure combining controlled temperature, humidity, and irrigation management.
- **Best for**: High-value crops such as bell peppers, exotic vegetables (like broccoli, lettuce, strawberries, and cherry tomatoes).
- **Features**:
    - Automated drip irrigation.
    - Solar-powered temperature regulation.
    - Fogging systems for humidity control.
    - Raised soil beds for better drainage.
