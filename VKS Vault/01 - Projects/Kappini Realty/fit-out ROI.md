## **1. Defining ROI in Fit-Outs**  
**Return on Investment (ROI)** measures the financial gain (or loss) generated from a fit-out relative to its cost.  

**Formula:**  
\[
ROI = \frac{\text{Net Benefit}}{\text{Total Cost}} \times 100
\]  
Where:  
- **Net Benefit** = Increased productivity + Employee retention + Rental savings  
- **Total Cost** = Fit-out expense + Maintenance

## **2. Key Financial Metrics to Evaluate**  
### **(A) Payback Period**  
Time taken to recover the fit-out cost from operational savings.  

**Formula:**  
\[
\text{Payback Period (Years)} = \frac{\text{Total Fit-Out Cost}}{\text{Annual Savings}}
\]  

### **(B) Net Present Value (NPV)**  
Todayâ€™s value of future cash flows (discounted at the firmâ€™s cost of capital).  

**Formula:**  
\[
NPV = \sum \left( \frac{\text{Cash Flow}_t}{(1 + r)^t} \right) - \text{Initial Investment}
\]  
*(Where \( r \) = discount rate, \( t \) = time period)*  

### **(C) Internal Rate of Return (IRR)**  
The discount rate that makes NPV = 0. Higher IRR = better investment.
