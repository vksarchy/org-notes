Tamil Nadu is actively expanding its network of Neo TIDEL Parks—mini IT parks designed to foster the growth of the IT and ITeS sectors in tier-2 and tier-3 cities. These facilities typically offer built-up areas ranging from 50,000 to 1 lakh square feet, tailored to meet local market demands citeturn0search20.

**Existing and Upcoming Neo TIDEL Parks:**

1. **Villupuram:** In February 2024, Chief Minister M.K. Stalin inaugurated the first Mini-TIDEL Park in Villupuram. This four-story facility spans 63,000 sq ft and was constructed at a cost of ₹31 crore citeturn0search26.

2. **Thanjavur:** The TIDEL Neo Park in Thanjavur was inaugurated in September 2024. Built on 3.4 acres, it offers 55,000 sq ft of office space across four stories. Due to high demand, there are plans to expand this facility further citeturn0search2.

3. **Salem:** Alongside Thanjavur, a Neo TIDEL Park was inaugurated in Salem in September 2024, providing similar facilities to support local IT growth citeturn0search25.

4. **Tiruppur:** The TIDEL Neo Park in Tiruppur is nearing completion, with civil works almost finished as of early October 2024. This seven-floor structure at Rakkipalayam village will offer 60,000 sq ft of built-up space citeturn0search21.

5. **Thoothukudi (Tuticorin):** Construction of the TIDEL Neo Park in Thoothukudi has been completed, and the facility is expected to be inaugurated soon citeturn0search2.

6. **Vellore:** A Neo TIDEL Park is planned for Vellore, with expectations for inauguration by the end of the year citeturn0search25.

In total, Tamil Nadu has initiated the development of Neo TIDEL Parks in at least seven districts: Villupuram, Thanjavur, Salem, Tiruppur, Thoothukudi, Vellore, and Madurai. These parks aim to decentralize IT development, creating employment opportunities and fostering economic growth across the state.
