## **Key Information Extracted**

### **1. Tax Reforms**
- **Income Tax Relief**: No income tax is payable for individuals earning up to ₹12 lakhs annually due to rebates.
- **Revised Tax Slabs**:
  - 0–4 lakhs: 0%
  - 4–8 lakhs: 5%
  - 8–12 lakhs: 10%
  - 12–16 lakhs: 15%
  - 16–20 lakhs: 20%
  - 20–24 lakhs: 25%
  - Above 24 lakhs: 30%
- **Impact on Middle Class**: Significant tax relief for middle-class citizens, increasing disposable income.

### **2. Sectoral Focus**
- **Consumer Goods and D2C Brands**:
  - Increased disposable income expected to boost demand for premium consumer goods, especially in Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities.
- **Agriculture**:
  - High-yield seed distribution, low-interest loans through Kisan Credit Cards, and modernization of cotton farming.
  - Special focus on makhana (fox nuts) production in Bihar with the establishment of a Makhana Board.
- **MSMEs and Startups**:
  - Increased loan guarantees for MSMEs (up to ₹10 crores) and startups (up to ₹20 crores).
  - Relaxed investment and turnover limits for MSME classification.
- **Manufacturing**:
  - Emphasis on expanding domestic manufacturing capabilities.

### **3. Criticisms**
- **Regional Bias**: Allegations of favoritism towards Bihar due to upcoming elections, with minimal focus on other states.
- **Fiscal Deficit Reduction Concerns**:
  - Achieved by cutting planned spending rather than increasing revenue.
- **Reduced Funding for Flagship Schemes**:
  - Programs like Jal Jeevan Mission and PM Garib Kalyan Anna Yojana reportedly received reduced funding.
