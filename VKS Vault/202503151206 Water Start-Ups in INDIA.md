[[Water Treatment]]

Several innovative water technology startups in India are working to address the country's water management challenges. Here are some notable examples:

## India-wide Startups

1. **Uravu Labs**: Founded in 2019 in Bengaluru, Uravu Labs has developed a 100% renewable technology that uses environmental moisture and solar thermal energy to create drinking water[1](https://yourstory.com/socialstory/2022/03/indian-startups-aiming-to-solve-water-crisis)[5](https://www.udayindia.in/news/6-startups-working-in-indias-water-sector).
    
2. **Swajal**: Established in 2014 in Kanpur, Swajal offers solar-powered water purification systems using ultra-filtration and reverse osmosis (RO) technology[1](https://yourstory.com/socialstory/2022/03/indian-startups-aiming-to-solve-water-crisis)[5](https://www.udayindia.in/news/6-startups-working-in-indias-water-sector).
    
3. **DigitalPaani**: Founded in 2020, DigitalPaani provides an IoT-enabled platform for water asset management, enhancing operational efficiency and reducing costs in industrial and municipal facilities[5](https://www.udayindia.in/news/6-startups-working-in-indias-water-sector).
    
4. **SmartOzean**: Based in Bengaluru and founded in 2018, SmartOzean focuses on IoT-enabled water management solutions, offering real-time monitoring of water quality and usage[4](https://startupchronicle.in/top-10-watertech-startups-in-india/).
    
5. **Maithri Aquatech**: This startup has developed MEGHDOOT technology, which generates water from air, providing decentralized and sustainable water solutions[2](https://www.ashirvad.com/news/25-water-tech-startups-reach-the-semi-finals-of-the-nudge-prize-ashirvad-water-challenge/).
    
## Tamil Nadu-based Startup

**WEGoT Utility Solutions**: Founded in 2015 and headquartered in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, WEGoT has developed a high-precision IoT-based water management system. Their solution helps reduce water consumption by up to 50% in large residential facilities and provides valuable data to stakeholders[5](https://www.udayindia.in/news/6-startups-working-in-indias-water-sector).

These startups are leveraging various technologies such as IoT, solar power, and atmospheric water generation to address India's water security challenges and promote sustainable water management practices.
