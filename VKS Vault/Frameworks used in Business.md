There are numerous frameworks used in business, strategy, and decision-making. These frameworks provide structured approaches to analyze situations, develop strategies, and make informed choices. Below are some commonly used ones, categorized by their purpose:

---

### **1. Business Strategy Frameworks**

#### **SWOT Analysis**

- **Purpose**: Assess internal strengths and weaknesses, and external opportunities and threats.
- **Use**: Strategic planning and risk management.

#### **Porter's Five Forces**

- **Purpose**: Analyze industry competition and profitability.
- **Use**: Industry analysis and market positioning.

#### **BCG Matrix (Growth-Share Matrix)**

- **Purpose**: Categorize products or business units based on market growth and market share.
- **Use**: Resource allocation and portfolio management.

#### **Ansoff Matrix**

- **Purpose**: Identify growth strategies based on products and markets (e.g., market penetration, diversification).
- **Use**: Growth planning and market expansion.

#### **Value Chain Analysis**

- **Purpose**: Analyze activities within a company to find cost savings or value-adding opportunities.
- **Use**: Competitive advantage development.

#### **Blue Ocean Strategy**

- **Purpose**: Identify uncontested market spaces to eliminate competition.
- **Use**: Innovation and differentiation.

---

### **2. Decision-Making Frameworks**

#### **Decision Matrix (Weighted Scoring Model)**

- **Purpose**: Evaluate and prioritize options based on multiple criteria.
- **Use**: Selecting vendors, products, or strategies.

#### **OODA Loop (Observe, Orient, Decide, Act)**

- **Purpose**: Enable quick, adaptive decision-making.
- **Use**: Competitive business environments.

#### **Six Thinking Hats**

- **Purpose**: Encourage different perspectives in decision-making.
- **Use**: Brainstorming and group discussions.

#### **Pareto Analysis (80/20 Rule)**

- **Purpose**: Identify the 20% of actions causing 80% of results.
- **Use**: Problem-solving and prioritization.

#### **Cost-Benefit Analysis**

- **Purpose**: Compare the benefits of an action to its costs.
- **Use**: Financial and strategic decision-making.

---

### **3. Operational Frameworks**

#### **Lean and Six Sigma**

- **Purpose**: Improve efficiency and reduce waste in processes.
- **Use**: Operational excellence.

#### **Theory of Constraints (TOC)**

- **Purpose**: Identify and address the most significant bottleneck in a process.
- **Use**: Process improvement and optimization.

#### **Balanced Scorecard**

- **Purpose**: Track performance across financial, customer, internal processes, and learning metrics.
- **Use**: Performance management.

#### **Kaizen**

- **Purpose**: Continuous improvement through small, incremental changes.
- **Use**: Operational improvement.

---

### **4. Innovation and Problem-Solving Frameworks**

#### **Design Thinking**

- **Purpose**: Solve problems through user-centric design and creativity.
- **Use**: Product development and innovation.

#### **TRIZ (Theory of Inventive Problem Solving)**

- **Purpose**: Systematic approach to solving technical problems.
- **Use**: Engineering and product design.

#### **SCAMPER**

- **Purpose**: Generate new ideas by modifying existing ones (Substitute, Combine, Adapt, etc.).
- **Use**: Creativity and brainstorming.

---

### **5. Financial and Investment Frameworks**

#### **DCF Analysis (Discounted Cash Flow)**

- **Purpose**: Estimate the value of an investment based on future cash flows.
- **Use**: Valuation and financial decision-making.

#### **CAPM (Capital Asset Pricing Model)**

- **Purpose**: Calculate expected return on an investment considering risk.
- **Use**: Portfolio management.

---

### **6. Organizational Strategy Frameworks**

#### **McKinsey 7S Framework**

- **Purpose**: Align organizational elements (Structure, Strategy, Systems, etc.).
- **Use**: Organizational change and strategy implementation.

#### **GE-McKinsey Matrix**

- **Purpose**: Evaluate business units based on market attractiveness and competitive strength.
- **Use**: Portfolio management.

#### **OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)**

- **Purpose**: Set and track measurable goals.
- **Use**: Performance alignment and goal setting.

---

### **7. Risk Management Frameworks**

#### **PESTLE Analysis**

- **Purpose**: Analyze Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, and Environmental factors.
- **Use**: Risk assessment and strategic planning.

#### **Risk Matrix**

- **Purpose**: Evaluate risks based on likelihood and impact.
- **Use**: Risk management.

---
