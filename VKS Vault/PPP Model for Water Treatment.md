### **Public-Private Partnership (PPP) in Water Management**

**Public-Private Partnership (PPP)** is a collaborative model where the government partners with private companies to **finance, develop, operate, and maintain** water infrastructure projects. This approach helps improve efficiency, reduce financial burden on the government, and bring in advanced technology.

---

## **PPP Models in Water Projects**

1. **Build-Operate-Transfer (BOT)** ‚Äì The private entity builds and operates the infrastructure for a set period before transferring it to the government.
2. **Design-Build-Operate (DBO)** ‚Äì The private sector designs, builds, and operates the project, while the government owns it.
3. **Hybrid Annuity Model (HAM)** ‚Äì A mix of government and private funding, where the government provides partial upfront support and the private player recovers the investment over time.
4. **O&M Contracts (Operations & Maintenance)** ‚Äì The private entity takes over the management and maintenance of existing water infrastructure.

---

## **Companies Using PPP in Water Projects (India & Global Use Cases)**

### **1. VA Tech Wabag (India)**

- **Chennai 400 MLD Tertiary Treatment Reverse Osmosis (TTRO) Plant**
    
    - Model: **DBO + 15-year O&M**
    - Treated wastewater is supplied to industries, reducing freshwater dependency.
    - First large-scale PPP wastewater reuse project in India.
- **Namami Gange Sewage Treatment Plants (STPs) ‚Äì Varanasi, Patna, etc.**
    
    - Model: **Hybrid Annuity (HAM) + 15-year O&M**
    - Government funds 40% upfront; private sector recovers the rest over time.

---

### **2. Suez (France) ‚Äì Mumbai Water Distribution Project**

- Model: **Performance-Based PPP**
- Suez manages the **water distribution system in Mumbai** to reduce leakages and ensure 24/7 water supply.
- Focus on **smart metering, reducing non-revenue water (NRW), and improving service quality**.

---

### **3. Manila Water (Philippines) ‚Äì Metro Manila Concession**

- Model: **Concession Model (25-year PPP contract)**
- Government handed over water supply and sewerage services to **private players (Manila Water & Maynilad)**.
- Resulted in **higher efficiency, better water supply, and reduced losses**.

---

### **4. Thames Water (UK) ‚Äì Thames Tideway Tunnel**

- Model: **BOT with Private Financing**
- A major sewage infrastructure project in London to reduce **river pollution**.
- Government provides partial risk guarantees to private investors.

---

### **5. Veolia (France) ‚Äì Nagpur 24x7 Water Supply**

- Model: **PPP for Distribution & O&M**
- Veolia ensures 24x7 water supply to Nagpur, reducing leakages and improving service quality.
- Focus on **smart water meters and digital monitoring**.
