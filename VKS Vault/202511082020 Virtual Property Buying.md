The best-known and most reputable companies in India enabling virtual property buying—where you can discover, tour, and even finalize land deals almost entirely online—include:

**1. NoBroker**
- Allows users to view, negotiate, and transact properties with minimal broker intervention. Highly discussed on Reddit, especially for its integrity in eliminating middlemen and for streamlining rentals and sales, though some users mention UI/UX limitations and a mixed experience with resale or plots.[1][2][3]

**2. MagicBricks**
- Renowned for digital tours, extensive listings, and strong support features. It also comes up frequently in forums as a reliable platform for both new and resale properties, though some users complain about the accuracy of listings and contactability.[4][2][1]

**3. 99Acres**
- Another trusted online real estate portal with advanced filtering, virtual tours, and legal assistance. Recommended on Reddit, especially for buyers seeking direct owners, resale deals, and transparency.[2][1]

**4. Settlin**
- Frequently mentioned on Reddit for virtual tours and helping with the entire end-to-end process, including documentation and visiting homes virtually. Users report Settlin is especially useful for urban resale properties.[5][1]

**5. Square Yards**
- Offers online and 3D guided tours, digital documentation, and virtual sales offices. Recognized in industry write-ups for its international reach and digital-first approach, with positive user testimonials on clarity and support during the end-to-end process.[6]

**6. Ivy Homes and Jumbo Homes**
- Newer proptech startups that focus on transparent online processes and accurate listings, highlighted by users on Reddit for honesty in listings, fair pricing, and helpful virtual tours—especially for ready-to-move, resale homes.[5]

**Key Reddit Insights:** 
- **NoBroker, Settlin, MagicBricks, and 99Acres** come up repeatedly in Reddit discussions—with users generally trusting these for transparency, online features, and less hassle for remote or first-time buyers.
- Smaller, newer startups like **Ivy Homes and Jumbo Homes** receive niche praise for their pricing honesty and virtual walkthroughs in the resale segment.
- Some caution that while these platforms work well, there’s always value in physical verification before final commitment, suggesting they streamline—rather than totally replace—the process.
