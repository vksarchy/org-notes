Here’s a summary of the key **finance metrics** and related numbers mentioned in the video about India’s Chemical Sector:

**Sector Size & Growth:**

- Current sector size: **$220 billion** (₹18 lakh crore)
    
- Projected sector size by 2040: **$1 trillion** (with expected CAGR of **8.36%**)
    
- Contribution to GDP: **~7%**
    

**Breakdown — Bulk vs Specialty Chemicals:**

- Bulk chemicals: **80%** of India’s chemical market, EBITDA margins: **10–15%**
    
- Specialty chemicals: **20%** of the market, EBITDA margins: **20–25%**
    
- Specialty chemical segment is more profitable but capital and R&D-intensive
    

**Costs:**

- **Raw material cost:** **50–60%** of revenue; most significant cost factor
    
- **Power & fuel cost in manufacturing:** ~**12%** of revenue
    
- **Transportation/logistics cost:** **3–8%** of revenue; Tata Chemicals reported up to **18%**
    
- **Logistics cost for the sector:** National: **13–14% of GDP** (vs single-digit in other countries)
    

**Company-Specific Capex Announcements:**

- Aarti Industries: **₹1,000 crore capex** for FY26 (high-value, niche products, margin improvement focus)
    
- Deepak Nitrite: **₹8,500 crore capex** for expansion and advanced intermediaries
    
- Birla: Acquisition of US-based Cargill’s specialty chemical facility (capacity addition projections: **16,000 to 40,000 tons** in two years)
    
- Apical Ltd: Specialty chemical share in portfolio increased from **26% (FY22)** to **56% (FY25)**, projected **70% by FY28**
    

**R&D Spending:**

- Indian chemical companies spend only **2–3%** of revenue on R&D
    
- Global average: **9–10%**
    

**Import Dependency:**

- High dependence on imports for key raw materials (e.g., Sulphuric acid, Methanol, Acetic acid, Caustic soda)
    
- Example: Methanol imports cover **70%** of need; PI Industries: increase in domestic sourcing from **44% (FY23)** to **66% (FY24)**
    

**Employment/Wages:**

- Manufacturing wage (2023): **India - $196/month**; Vietnam - $323/month; China - $128/month
    

**Sector Challenges & Risks:**

- Raw material price volatility (due to import dependency and currency fluctuations)
    
- Low R&D investment
    
- High logistics cost and infrastructure bottlenecks
    
- Competition and oversupply from China (global price pressure, margin squeeze)
    
- Sector is cyclical, tied to global economic trends
    

**Export Data:**

- India in global top 10 for chemical exports
    
- Specialty chemicals: India is a net exporter, with room for growth if even **20% of China’s supply chain shifts** here
