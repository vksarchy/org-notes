Tamil people are familiar with several types of millets, known as "siruthaniyangal" in Tamil. Here's a list of millets commonly known and used in Tamil cuisine:

1. Kelvaragu (கேழ்வரகு) - Finger Millet (Ragi)[2](https://standardcoldpressedoil.com/shop/sirudhaniyam-names-in-tamil)[4](https://www.tomatoblues.com/millet-names-in-different-languages/)
    
2. Kambu (கம்பு) - Pearl Millet (Bajra)[2](https://standardcoldpressedoil.com/shop/sirudhaniyam-names-in-tamil)[4](https://www.tomatoblues.com/millet-names-in-different-languages/)
    
3. Samai (சாமை) - Little Millet[2](https://standardcoldpressedoil.com/shop/sirudhaniyam-names-in-tamil)[4](https://www.tomatoblues.com/millet-names-in-different-languages/)
    
4. Thinai (தினை) - Foxtail Millet[2](https://standardcoldpressedoil.com/shop/sirudhaniyam-names-in-tamil)[4](https://www.tomatoblues.com/millet-names-in-different-languages/)
    
5. Varagu (வரகு) - Kodo Millet[2](https://standardcoldpressedoil.com/shop/sirudhaniyam-names-in-tamil)[4](https://www.tomatoblues.com/millet-names-in-different-languages/)
    
6. Kuthiraivali (குதிரைவாலி) - Barnyard Millet[2](https://standardcoldpressedoil.com/shop/sirudhaniyam-names-in-tamil)[3](https://thatscurry.com/millet-dishes-as-part-of-tamil-curry-tradition/)
    
7. Panivaragu (பனிவரகு) - Proso Millet[2](https://standardcoldpressedoil.com/shop/sirudhaniyam-names-in-tamil)[4](https://www.tomatoblues.com/millet-names-in-different-languages/)
    
8. Cholam (சோளம்) - Sorghum (Great Millet)[2](https://standardcoldpressedoil.com/shop/sirudhaniyam-names-in-tamil)[4](https://www.tomatoblues.com/millet-names-in-different-languages/)
    
9. Pala Pul (பாலா புல்) - Brown Top Millet[2](https://standardcoldpressedoil.com/shop/sirudhaniyam-names-in-tamil)
    

These millets are integral to Tamil cuisine and are used in various traditional dishes, including porridges, dosas, and biryanis[3](https://thatscurry.com/millet-dishes-as-part-of-tamil-curry-tradition/). They are valued for their nutritional benefits and are often referred to collectively as "siruthaniyam" or "siruthaniyangal" in Tamil[2](https://standardcoldpressedoil.com/shop/sirudhaniyam-names-in-tamil).
