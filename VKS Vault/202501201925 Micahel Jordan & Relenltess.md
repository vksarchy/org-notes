# How to develop a relentless mindset like michael jordan? Why do i side towards nihilsm. Am I not comfortable with failure?

  
Developing a Relentless Mindset Like Michael Jordan

**1. ** Embrace Failure as Feedback:

- Michael Jordan famously said, "I've failed over and over again in my life. And that is why I succeed." View every failure not as a defeat but as feedback. Learn from what went wrong, adapt, and approach the next challenge with this new knowledge.
    

**2. ** Set Clear, High Goals:

- Jordan was known for setting incredibly high goals for himself. Define what relentless means for you, whether in sports, work, or personal life. Set goals that push your limits.
  

**3. ** Unwavering Work Ethic:

- His work ethic was legendary. Dedicate yourself to practice and improvement. The more you put in, the more you get out. This doesn't mean working without rest but working smart, with focus and intensity.
      

**4. ** Mental Toughness:

- Develop resilience through mental conditioning. Techniques like visualization, meditation, or even talking through scenarios can prepare you for high-pressure situations. Jordan used visualization to see his success before it happened.
    

**5. ** Competitive Drive:

- Find what fuels your competitive spirit. Jordan thrived on competition, even in practice. Understand what motivates you to push harder.
  

**6. ** Surround Yourself with Excellence:

- Being around people who challenge and inspire you can elevate your performance. Jordan benefited from teammates who pushed him and coaches who demanded greatness.
  

**7. ** Never Stop Learning:

- Even at the peak of his career, Jordan was learning. Adopt a mindset where you're always open to new strategies, skills, or perspectives.

# Why You Might Side Towards Nihilism

**1. ** Fear of Failure:

- If you're uncomfortable with failure, nihilism might seem like an escape where nothing matters, so failure has no consequence. It’s a defense mechanism against potential disappointment or pain.
    


**2. ** Existential Overwhelm:

- The vastness of human existence and the universe can be daunting, leading to feelings of meaninglessness. This overwhelming sense can push one towards nihilistic thoughts as a way to cope.
    


**3. ** Lack of Purpose or Direction:

- Without clear goals or a sense of purpose, life can seem pointless, leading to nihilistic views. If you feel aimless, this can be a significant factor.
    


**4. ** Response to Modernity:

- The rapid pace of change, information overload, and societal pressures can make traditional sources of meaning (religion, community, etc.) feel obsolete, pushing some towards nihilism.
    


**5. ** Depression or Mental Health Issues:

- Sometimes, nihilism can be a symptom of underlying mental health issues like depression, where one's perception of the world becomes bleak.
    
# Addressing Comfort with Failure:
 

- Reframe Failure: See it as a step towards success, not an endpoint.
    
- Exposure: Gradually expose yourself to situations where you might fail but in controlled, low-stakes environments to build resilience.
    
- Celebrate Learning: Focus on what you learned from each experience rather than the outcome itself.
    
- Therapy or Counseling: If nihilism or fear of failure is significantly impacting your life, professional help can offer tools to manage these feelings.
    

  

Developing a relentless mindset like Michael Jordan involves embracing all aspects of your journey, including failure, as part of the path to greatness. Addressing why you might lean towards nihilism involves understanding your relationship with failure, purpose, and existential questions. Both journeys require introspection, action, and often, a shift in perspective.
