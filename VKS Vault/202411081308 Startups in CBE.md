### 3. **PharmEasy**
   - **Focus**: Online pharmacy and healthcare services.
   - **What They Do**: Delivers medicines, healthcare products, and diagnostic services to consumers at their doorstep.
   - **Impact**: PharmEasy has made healthcare more accessible by offering discounted medicines and home diagnostic services.

### 4. **SigTuple**
   - **Focus**: Medical diagnostics using AI.
   - **What They Do**: Develops AI-powered tools for automated analysis of medical data such as blood samples, urine samples, and X-rays.
   - **Impact**: SigTuple’s AI technology helps in faster diagnosis and reduces human error in pathology.

### 5. **Niramai**
   - **Focus**: Breast cancer screening.
   - **What They Do**: Uses AI-based thermal imaging for early detection of breast cancer.
   - **Impact**: Niramai offers a non-invasive, radiation-free method for early cancer detection, making it more accessible to women in rural areas.

### 6. **HealthifyMe**
   - **Focus**: Digital health and wellness.
   - **What They Do**: Provides personalized diet plans, workout routines, and health tracking through an app.
   - **Impact**: HealthifyMe uses AI-based virtual assistants to offer customized health solutions to users.

### 7. **1mg**
   - **Focus**: Online pharmacy and healthcare platform.
   - **What They Do**: Offers medicine delivery, lab tests at home, and detailed information about medicines.
   - **Impact**: 1mg has simplified access to healthcare products and services by providing them online at competitive prices.

### 8. **MedGenome**
   - **Focus**: Genomics-based diagnostics.
   - **What They Do**: Provides genetic testing services for various diseases including cancer, cardiovascular diseases, and rare genetic disorders.
   - **Impact**: MedGenome is a leader in personalized medicine through its advanced genomics solutions.

These startups are playing a crucial role in transforming the healthcare landscape in India by leveraging technology to make healthcare more accessible, affordable, and efficient.

### Agriculture

Tamil Nadu, particularly Coimbatore, has seen a significant rise in startups across various sectors, including **agriculture**. According to the attached image, **7.5%** of the startups in Coimbatore are focused on the **Agriculture** sector. Below are some notable agri-tech startups from Tamil Nadu:

### 1. **WayCool Foods**
   - **Location**: Chennai, Tamil Nadu
   - **What They Do**: WayCool is a food and agri-tech platform that focuses on streamlining the supply chain for fresh produce, dairy, and staples. It uses technology to optimize the farm-to-fork supply chain.
   - **Impact**: By leveraging data analytics and automation, WayCool helps farmers get better prices and reduces food wastage.

### 2. **Kamal Kisan**
   - **Location**: Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu
   - **What They Do**: Kamal Kisan develops affordable and efficient farm mechanization tools tailored for small and marginal farmers.
   - **Impact**: Their products help reduce labor costs and increase productivity for farmers, making farming more sustainable.

### 3. **FarmGuru**
   - **Location**: Chennai, Tamil Nadu
   - **What They Do**: FarmGuru is an agri-tech startup that helps farmers access high-quality seeds, fertilizers, and equipment at affordable prices through its online platform.
   - **Impact**: It simplifies access to essential farming inputs and helps farmers increase their crop yields.

### 4. **Aibono**
   - **Location**: Udhagamandalam (Ooty), Tamil Nadu
   - **What They Do**: Aibono uses AI and predictive analytics to help farmers optimize their crop production based on market demand.
   - **Impact**: Aibono’s platform helps reduce overproduction and ensures that farmers grow crops that are in demand, leading to better profitability.

### 5. **AgNext**
   - **Location**: Chennai, Tamil Nadu
   - **What They Do**: AgNext focuses on quality assessment of agricultural commodities using AI-based solutions. It provides real-time data on crop quality to help farmers and buyers make informed decisions.
   - **Impact**: AgNext’s technology ensures transparency in the agricultural supply chain by providing accurate quality assessments.

These startups are leveraging technology to address key challenges in agriculture such as supply chain inefficiencies, lack of mechanization, and market unpredictability. With the support of incubation centers and government initiatives like the Tamil Nadu Startup and Innovation Policy (as highlighted in the image), these startups are poised for further growth.
