Yes, **snap ambush** is a real military concept. It refers to an **immediate, unplanned ambush** set up on the spot in response to an emerging threat or tactical opportunity. It's usually executed when a unit unexpectedly encounters enemy forces and needs to quickly establish a hasty ambush position.

### Key Features:

- **Rapid execution** – No time for elaborate setup.
- **Short-duration** – Engaged only as long as needed.
- **Fire superiority** – Overwhelm before the enemy reacts.
- **Immediate disengagement** – Break contact if necessary.

It’s commonly used in **small-unit tactics** by infantry, special operations forces, and guerrilla fighters.
