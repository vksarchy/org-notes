# Enemy: 
### **General Terms for Enemy Forces:**

✅ **Tango** – Target (often used for individual enemies, e.g., "Tango down")  
✅ **Hostile** – Confirmed enemy combatant  
✅ **OpFor (Opposing Force)** – Used in training exercises for simulated enemies  
✅ **Threat** – Broad term for enemy presence or danger  
✅ **Bandit** – Enemy aircraft (used in air combat)  
✅ **Bogey** – Unidentified aircraft (may or may not be hostile)  
✅ **Aggressor** – Adversary force in combat or training

### **Tactical Slang & Informal Terms:**

✅ **Bad guys** – General slang for enemy forces  
✅ **Hajji** – Sometimes used in Middle Eastern conflicts (can be offensive)  
✅ **Dicker** – Enemy spotter or scout  
✅ **Sniffer** – Enemy reconnaissance element  
✅ **Zombies** – Used in air combat to describe hostile missiles or aircraft

### **Radio Calls & Codewords (Mission Dependent):**

✅ **Red Team** – Enemy force in training simulations  
✅ **Black Element** – Can refer to adversarial units in operations  
✅ **Unfriendlies** – Another term for enemy presence

# Hostage: 

Bravo 1, here’s how you refer to **hostages** and **civilians** in military lingo:

### **Hostage Terms:**

✅ **POW (Prisoner of War)** – Captured military personnel  
✅ **Detainee** – Captured individual, not necessarily a POW  
✅ **HVT (High-Value Target)** – If the hostage is a critical asset  
✅ **PAX (Passengers)** – Can be used for hostages in certain situations  
✅ **Package** – Sometimes used in covert ops for a hostage to be extracted  
✅ **Friendly Held Captive** – If the hostage is allied personnel  
✅ **Kilo (K)** – Codeword for a hostage in some ops

**Example in comms:**

- _"We have three PAX held hostage at the objective."_
- _"Mission is to extract the package from hostile control."_
- _"Intel suggests an HVT is among the detainees."_

---

### **Civilian Terms:**

✅ **Civvies** – Short for civilians  
✅ **Non-combatants** – Civilians in a conflict zone  
✅ **Soft targets** – Unarmed or unprotected civilians (used in threat assessment)  
✅ **HUMINT Source** – If a civilian provides intelligence  
✅ **IDP (Internally Displaced Person)** – Civilians fleeing conflict within their country  
✅ **Refugee** – Civilians fleeing across borders due to conflict  
✅ **Lima (L)** – Sometimes used as a code for local civilians  
✅ **Local Nationals (LN)** – Civilians native to the operational area

**Example in comms:**

- _"Civvies in the AO, check fire."_
- _"Extracting non-combatants from the danger zone."_
- _"Friendly forces providing aid to LNs."_
